Project Overview

Pyre provides a life-saving communication tool for vulnerable communities during wildfire crises by delivering satellite-based evacuation alerts to households without internet or cellular access.

As product owner and lead designer, I defined the product vision, led end-to-end research and design, and aligned our cross-functional team on a two-day roadmap to deliver a high-impact MVP ‚Äî combining hardware design, system mapping, and UI prototyping for emergency management operations.

Problem

75% of rural Californians live in high-risk wildfire zones without reliable broadband or cellular service ‚Äî making digital alerts inaccessible and evacuation timelines dangerously slow.

As climate-driven wildfires grow in intensity, local governments face limited tools to notify residents in real time. Current systems like text alerts and emergency apps depend on connectivity, leaving low-income and elderly residents most vulnerable.

Product Goal

Develop a scalable, offline alert system that empowers emergency agencies to send targeted evacuation warnings to disconnected communities ‚Äî without relying on digital infrastructure.

Our product needed to meet three criteria: resilient delivery, low learning curve, and municipal deployability. Within 6 hours, I scoped and designed an MVP that achieved functional proof-of-concept across each.

Research & Discovery

I analyzed wildfire and broadband access data from CAL FIRE, FEMA, and the U.S. Census Bureau, and interviewed three emergency management professionals to validate feasibility. I synthesized insights into key product requirements.

Product Strategy & MVP Scope

I led scoping decisions to define a feasible MVP that would demonstrate end-to-end functionality: from agency dashboard ‚Üí satellite ‚Üí household receiver.

1.

Dashboard Interface: Allows agencies to draw evacuation zones, preview messages, and send alerts via satellite uplink.

2.

Radio Receiver Device: Emits siren and voice-based alert signals using satellite-triggered activation.

3.

Data Integration: Pulls from real-time CAL FIRE data to preemptively identify high-risk zones.

By defining these as the minimum viable components, I ensured the product demo could simulate a complete emergency workflow ‚Äî validating concept-market fit.
